Counterpoint was founded in 2000 by Carolyn Weger as Carnegie Mellon’s first all-female a cappella group. We are a fun group of girls who love to sing and harmonize and beatbox and jam out on stage and during rehearsals. We sing everything from pop to barbershop to folk.

In 2008, we founded the A CaPittsburgh benefit concert, and we have been holding concerts to benefit local causes and non-profits that are related to music every year since then. We have also been competing at the International Championship of Collegiate A Cappella (ICCA) annually. Some of our other concerts at CMU include No Instruments Aloud, the Carnival A Cappella Concert, and Almost Midnight A Cappella. You can also catch us in Morewood’s Underground stage singing for Sleeping Bag Weekends. We sing at some events in the community as well, and our favorite is singing at Phipps during the holiday season!

We also do singing valentines every February, where we do a live serenade or singing phone call to a person of your choice. It’s super fun to surprise a special someone or embarrass a friend! If you would like to have Counterpoint sing at an event or for someone’s birthday, please contact us at countrpt@andrew.cmu.edu and we will see if we can accommodate you!

    Sign up for an audition slot here!
    What to Prepare:

    Please prepare a short solo piece to sing for us!

    What to Expect:

    When you arrive at the audition, we will start with a short introduction of our current members. We will start with a warm up exercise going up and down to determine your range. Next, we will ask you to sing your prepared song selection. After that, we will give you about a minute to look over a short sight reading exercise. We will give you a starting pitch and you'll sing back the notes at whatever tempo is comfortable for you. (We recommend taking it slowly!) Lastly, we will let you look over a rhythm exercise and we ask that you clap or tap the rhythm back for us at any tempo of your choice. And that's it!
